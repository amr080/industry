1）Weekly RWAfi Highlights

ParaFi’s Bold 2030 Predictions: RWAs and Stablecoins

ParaFi forecasts two major shifts by 2030: tokenized real-world assets (RWAs) could surpass digital assets in value, and stablecoins might reach 10% of the U.S. M2 money supply. RWAs, currently valued at $13.5 billion, are growing rapidly with tokenized treasuries leading the way. Stablecoins, already driving $302 billion in weekly transfer volume, are poised to disrupt global payment systems and fintech. Both trends highlight blockchain's potential to revolutionize finance.

Read more: blockworks.co

Ondo Finance Brings USDY Multichain with LayerZero

Ondo Finance has integrated LayerZero’s Omnichain Fungible Token (OFT) standard to make its USDY yieldcoin fungible across Ethereum, Mantle, and Arbitrum, with plans for more networks. USDY, backed by bank deposits and Treasury bills, offers a 4.9% APR and has over $450 million in total value locked. This move positions USDY as an alternative to stablecoins like USDC and USDT, enabling seamless transfers without liquidity pools or asset wrapping.

Read more: cointelegraph.com

The Rebirth of RWA Tokenization in 2024

RWA tokenization is booming, with onchain assets surpassing $13 billion in 2024. Improved U.S. regulations, bullish market conditions, and technological readiness have sparked exponential growth, with predictions of a $2–30 trillion market by 2030. Key players like Hedera and Tether are driving innovation, while trends like process tokenization and flexible compliance are reshaping the sector. Experts highlight tokenization’s potential to streamline traditional finance and unlock global liquidity, positioning RWAs as a game-changer for DeFi and TradFi alike.

Read more: cryptonews.com

Tokenized U.S. Treasury Market Hits $2.5 Billion, RWA Assets Surge Past $13 Billion

As of November 25, tokenized U.S. Treasury securities reached $2.5 billion in market size, reflecting growing investor interest in blockchain-powered financial instruments. The on-chain value of tokenized real-world assets (RWAs) also exceeded $13.4 billion, highlighting blockchain's transformative role in increasing liquidity, transparency, and accessibility. With digital asset adoption accelerating, blockchain is reshaping traditional finance, offering efficient, secure infrastructure for asset management and transactions.

Read more: binance.com

Ripple Launches Tokenized Money Market Fund on XRP Ledger

Ripple has announced the tokenization of a $4.77 billion money market fund on its XRP Ledger in partnership with Archax and Abrdn. This marks a major step in real-world asset (RWA) tokenization, leveraging blockchain for efficiency and security. Ripple plans to invest $5 million in Abrdn's Lux fund tokens, aiming to expand RWA offerings on XRPL. With tokenized assets projected to reach $16 trillion by 2030, Ripple positions itself as a key player in this evolving financial infrastructure.

2）RWA Market Commentary

1. Growth and Institutional Adoption

The real-world asset (RWA) market is experiencing unprecedented growth, with on-chain assets surpassing $13 billion in 2024 and tokenized U.S. Treasury securities alone reaching $2.5 billion. Institutional players like Abrdn, Ripple, and Ondo Finance are leading the charge, leveraging blockchain to tokenize assets such as money market funds and treasuries. These developments are reshaping traditional finance by enhancing liquidity, transparency, and accessibility while drawing in major institutions eager to capitalize on blockchain's efficiency. Analysts predict the tokenized asset market could exceed $16 trillion by 2030, signaling massive long-term potential.

2. Challenges and Opportunities in RWA Implementation

While the potential for RWAs is enormous, the sector faces hurdles such as regulatory uncertainty, social acceptance, and technical integration. Key players like Boson Protocol and Centrifuge stress the importance of overcoming regulatory challenges to accelerate adoption. However, opportunities abound, as governments and institutions increasingly acknowledge the benefits of blockchain-powered asset management. Ripple's collaboration with Archax and Abrdn demonstrates how partnerships can overcome barriers, providing blueprints for scalable RWA adoption.

3. Sector-Specific Dynamics

  • Financial Products: Tokenized treasuries dominate the space, accounting for $2.5 billion in market size. Products like USDY from Ondo Finance, offering 4.9% APR, present strong alternatives to stablecoins for yield-focused investors.
  • Real Estate: Platforms like RedSwan are tokenizing high-value assets such as real estate, unlocking efficiencies of up to 40%.
  • Money Markets: Ripple’s tokenized money market fund represents a new frontier for RWAs, demonstrating their versatility and appeal across asset classes.
  • Stablecoins: The interplay between stablecoins and RWAs is growing, with stablecoins driving $302 billion in weekly transfer volume. Analysts predict they could reach 10% of the U.S. M2 money supply by 2030.

4. Emerging Trends and Future Outlook

The RWA sector is evolving rapidly, driven by trends like process tokenization, which shifts focus from digitizing assets to reimagining workflows on-chain. Flexible compliance systems are enabling jurisdiction-specific tokenization while enhancing regulatory adaptability. As institutional capital flows into the space, the convergence of DeFi and TradFi is accelerating, with RWAs acting as a critical bridge. Looking ahead, the rise of "computable economies," as envisioned by Boson Protocol, and the broader adoption of blockchain infrastructures like Ripple’s XRPL, promise to redefine financial markets globally. By 2030, tokenization is expected to be a cornerstone of both traditional and decentralized finance ecosystems.
